Boeing 737-800 (IATA CODE: N36272)

This Boeing 737-800 (IATA Code: N36272), previously operated by United Airlines, has been in service for 14 years, from 2009 to 2023. Over its operational lifetime, it accumulated 35,000 flight hours and completed 14,500 flight cycles. The aircraft was maintained to FAA Part 121 standards, ensuring top-notch performance and reliability. It underwent its last major overhaul, a C-check, in 2022, making it ready for continued service. This aircraft's combination of performance, regular maintenance, and well-documented history makes it a reliable and cost-effective choice for future operators.

Airbus A340-300 (IATA CODE: HB-JMK)

The Airbus A340-300 with IATA code HB-JMK was part of Swiss International Air Lines' fleet, serving in long-haul operations for nearly two decades. Originally delivered to Swissair, the aircraft continued its service after the airline's transition to SWISS. Known for its range and comfort, the A340-300 was used on intercontinental routes connecting Europe to destinations in North America, Asia, and Africa. The aircraft had a maximum capacity of around 270 passengers and was appreciated for its spacious cabin layout.
